Title: Do you have a “third place”? Here’s why finding one is key for your well-being

Level: B2 (adapted text)

This lesson uses a simplified version of the text so upper-intermediate students can focus on key ideas, vocabulary and discussion.

Reading Comprehension

Read the adapted text. Then answer the questions. When you click Reveal answer, the answer appears in the white box and the exact evidence is highlighted in the text.

At the end of the day, many people go straight between home and work or school. But experts say that life may feel better if people also have a “third place”.

A third place is a place that is not your home and not your workplace or school. It can be almost any place: a coffee shop, a park, a bookstore, a church or a community centre. The important point is that you choose to go there. Nobody forces you to be there.

The idea of the third place comes from sociologist Ray Oldenburg, who wrote about it in 1989. He explained that these places help people relax in public and enjoy social life outside home and work. In third places, people often meet regular visitors and sometimes make new connections. Conversation is usually light and friendly, and there is no pressure to be productive.

According to researcher Narae Lee, one of the most important features of third places is social contact. This contact can be direct, such as chatting or doing activities with other people. It can also be indirect. For example, a person in a theatre may not speak to others, but simply being surrounded by people with a similar interest can still feel meaningful.

Third places matter because many societies are becoming lonelier. Even when people are quiet in a third place, simply being around others can reduce loneliness and help build a sense of community. Some people go just to enjoy the atmosphere, listen to the background noise and feel less alone.

How can people find a third place? Lee says the first step is to think about your interests. If you like reading, you could join a book club. If you enjoy exercise, a running club might work. Even a park can be a third place if you like being outdoors. The best third place is one that is easy to reach in daily life, on foot or by public transport. Over time, it may become somewhere you look forward to visiting.

Questions

1. What is a “third place”?

A third place is somewhere that is not your home and not your workplace or school.

2. Why are third places different from work or school?

You choose to go there; nobody forces you to be there.

3. What social benefit can third places provide?

They help people meet regular visitors and sometimes make new connections.

4. How can someone experience social contact without speaking directly to anyone?

They can simply be surrounded by people with a similar interest.

5. What makes a third place easier to include in everyday life?

It should be easy to reach, ideally on foot or by public transport.

Grammar: Defining and non-defining relative clauses

Examples from the topic
A third place is a location that is not your home or workplace.
Coffee shops, which are often easy to access, can be good third places.
A book club is a group whose members share an interest.

Use defining relative clauses to give essential information. Use non-defining relative clauses to add extra information. Non-defining clauses need commas.
